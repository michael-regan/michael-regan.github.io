About myself

    I work with professor Yejin Choi studying cognitive semantic approaches to the modeling of causal structure in language. My research focuses on creating datasets for model evaluation across a variety of tasks (e.g., causal schema induction, causal reasoning using LLMs), on problems in event, narrative, and dialogue understanding, and on functional approaches to studies of language which hold that physical models of the world shape both human cognition (e.g., intuitive physics) and linguistic constructions, form-meaning pairs where meaning is viewed as causal. I have a PhD in Linguistics (computational linguistic methods for causal structure discovery), with a MSc in Computer Science (with emphasis on machine learning and experimental methods in CS), and did internships at Amazon (Seattle and Boston) and Sandia National Laboratories.
